# Scrivener Stage 2 Fixture Acquisition And Evidence Expansion Plan

## Status

Governance and planning note only.

This note converts the current Stage 2 planning packet into a bounded fixture and evidence program.
It does not authorize Stage 2 implementation.
It does not authorize extraction.
It does not admit the Scrivener lane.

## Purpose

This note defines the minimum additional fixture classes, acquisition order, derivative strategy, provenance rules, and blocking findings needed to expand Scrivener Stage 2 evidence without creating implementation momentum.

It exists so fixture growth remains question-driven and fail closed rather than opportunistic.

## Binding posture

Current repo truth remains:

- Cortex is live through Slice 10.
- admitted lanes remain `md`, `txt`, `pdf`, `docx`, `rtf`, `odt`, and `epub`
- Scrivener remains unadmitted
- only the bounded Stage 1 authority-recon slice is implemented
- Stage 2 planning is open, but the Stage 2 implementation gate is closed
- the active Scrivener evidence packet is still limited to three clean sanitized derivatives, three sanitized-derived negative packets, and one superseded raw-derived audit packet
- current runtime and test evidence proves only Stage 1 status-only authority behavior across clean `ready`, malformed authority, multi-authority, directly missing correspondence, unsupported input, operator-disabled posture, and service-status reporting

That runtime and test evidence does not prove extraction truth, manuscript eligibility, compatibility breadth, deterministic mapping across item types, or lane admission readiness.

## 1. Minimum Additional Fixture Classes

The next evidence step is not "more Scrivener projects" in the abstract.
It is the smallest non-overlapping set of fixture classes that pressures the unresolved Stage 2 questions already named in the current control surface.

| Fixture class | Minimum additional acquisition target | Primary unresolved question it must pressure | Why the current packet is still insufficient |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| compatibility or migrated project coverage | at least one clean sanitized baseline from a newer, migrated, or otherwise non-current lineage with explicit platform and version provenance | do singular authority and candidate mapping observations survive version drift or migration rather than only the current Windows-shaped `SCRWIN-3.1.x` / `Version="2.0"` slice? | current clean evidence remains narrow, Windows-shaped, and lineage-limited |
| manuscript-heavy project | at least one clean sanitized baseline whose draft-side population is dense enough to study repeated text-bearing patterns, order, and item-identity pressure with low research noise | can candidate manuscript-side ordering, identity, and correspondence be observed repeatedly without mixed-surface ambiguity? | current positives are mixed and still leave manuscript-side patterns under-isolated |
| research-heavy project | at least one clean sanitized baseline whose text-bearing population is primarily non-draft and research-side | can research or workspace exclusion remain structural even when non-draft surfaces are text-rich? | current evidence shows role surfaces, but not enough pressure against convenience-based inclusion |
| mixed manuscript plus research boundary-pressure project | at least one additional clean sanitized baseline from a different lineage than the current mixed fixture, with both draft-side and non-draft text-bearing items and any explicit bookmark or template pressure preserved | do mixed populations force convenience heuristics, or can manuscript boundary remain explicit across more than one lineage? | one mixed baseline is not enough, especially with sanitized asset removal and narrow lineage spread |
| structurally divergent binder or item-type project | at least one clean sanitized baseline with deeper nesting, folder pressure, reordered items, or broader observed item-type mix than the current packet | is binder-to-content mapping deterministic enough across hierarchy shapes and item classes without semantic guessing? | current mapping proof is partial and concentrated in a narrow observed shape |
| tricky non-manuscript surface pressure project | at least one clean sanitized baseline that preserves project notes, synopsis, snapshots, bookmarks, templates, search, or other text-like auxiliary surfaces when they exist | can auxiliary and non-manuscript surfaces remain excluded without hiding dependencies or drifting into semantic inference? | the current packet shows some auxiliary variance, but not enough to retire hidden-dependency risk |
| degraded or partial correspondence family | at least one same-source clean baseline from the new Wave 1 or Wave 2 set that can support controlled negative derivatives for missing targets, partial-package states, and mixed-validity populations | can degraded states be represented truthfully, or do they continue to force `unavailable` even after evidence growth? | the current negative set covers only malformed authority, multi-authority, and one direct missing-content case |

The current packet partially informs several of these classes.
It does not retire any of them.

## 2. Priority Waves

Fixture acquisition should proceed in waves so the repo learns the highest-value blocking facts first and does not create derivative sprawl before the clean baseline questions are stable.

### Wave 1: compatibility and structural divergence pressure

Acquire first:

- compatibility or migrated project coverage
- structurally divergent binder or item-type coverage
- an additional mixed manuscript plus research baseline only if it also broadens lineage or hierarchy shape

Why this wave comes first:

- if authority, mapping, or item-type behavior collapses outside the current lineage, later boundary and degraded-case analysis will still be overfit to the present packet
- if compatibility remains too narrow, later manuscript-policy drafting still lacks a trustworthy evidence floor

Wave 1 exit posture:

- new clean baselines exist with explicit provenance and sanitization notes
- no implementation authority opens
- no compatibility claim is inferred beyond the exact observed lineages

### Wave 2: manuscript and non-manuscript boundary pressure

Acquire second:

- manuscript-heavy baseline
- research-heavy baseline
- tricky non-manuscript surface baseline
- any remaining mixed manuscript plus research boundary fixture not already covered in Wave 1

Why this wave comes after Wave 1:

- clean boundary evidence is most useful only after the repo knows whether the underlying authority and mapping assumptions survive broader lineage and structure pressure
- boundary rules cannot be narrowed honestly if the repo still does not know whether different project shapes preserve the same authority and correspondence conventions

Wave 2 exit posture:

- candidate manuscript-side and non-manuscript-side pressure has been observed across more than one lineage
- exclusions are still planning-only unless separate governance later says more
- Stage 2 implementation remains blocked

### Wave 3: degraded and mixed-validity pressure

Acquire or derive last:

- same-source missing-correspondence derivatives from the most useful Wave 1 and Wave 2 baselines
- partial-package or missing-auxiliary derivatives
- mixed-validity population derivatives
- item-type-specific structural irregularity derivatives

Why this wave comes last:

- negative evidence is only useful when the clean baseline already makes the target scope explicit
- without clean scope, degraded derivatives multiply ambiguity instead of retiring it
- Wave 3 is where the repo learns whether a truthful degraded Stage 2 model is even possible, not where it presumes one

Wave 3 exit posture:

- the repo can review whether degraded states are countable and isolatable
- the repo still does not gain implementation authority automatically
- any one blocking finding is enough to keep Stage 2 closed

Completion of any wave is evidence growth only.
It is not lane admission, extraction authorization, or Stage 2 implementation clearance.

## 3. Baseline-To-Derivative Program

Do not create every derivative from every baseline.
Create only the smallest derivative set that pressures the baseline's unique unresolved question while preserving same-source lineage clarity.

| Useful baseline class | Default derivative set | Unresolved question pressured |
| --- | --- | --- |
| compatibility or migrated baseline | malformed authority, multi-authority conflict, and missing auxiliary or correspondence targets when the clean baseline exposes text-bearing items | do fail-closed authority and dependency assumptions survive version drift or migration? |
| manuscript-heavy baseline | missing correspondence target, mixed-validity population, and hierarchy-sensitive degradation when the clean baseline has meaningful nesting | can candidate manuscript-side scope remain truthful when only part of the draft-side population degrades? |
| research-heavy baseline | research-side missing-target derivative, auxiliary-loss derivative, and item-type-specific irregularity when non-draft text-bearing items are present | does non-manuscript exclusion stay structural even when research-side items look text-capable? |
| mixed manuscript plus research baseline | paired missing-target derivatives across one draft-side item and one non-draft item, plus mixed-validity population pressure | do boundary rules survive mixed populations without convenience-based inclusion? |
| structurally divergent binder or item-type baseline | item-type-specific structural irregularity, multi-authority conflict, and missing-target derivative at non-trivial hierarchy depth | does mapping break on deep nesting, folder pressure, or wider item-type mix? |
| tricky non-manuscript surface baseline | missing auxiliary or sidecar derivatives created one class at a time, plus mixed-validity cases where auxiliaries remain but candidate text items degrade or vice versa | which auxiliary losses are harmless exclusions and which reveal hidden dependencies? |
| any Wave 1 or Wave 2 baseline promoted into degraded-family evidence | partial-package copy, multiple missing targets, and mixed-validity population | can degraded scope be counted and isolated honestly, or must Stage 2 stay `unavailable`? |

### Canonical derivative classes

The default derivative vocabulary should stay narrow:

- malformed authority: pressure whether fail-closed authority handling survives new lineage and version shapes
- multi-authority conflict: pressure whether ambiguous authority remains fail closed outside the current narrow packet
- missing correspondence targets: pressure whether direct missing candidate text must remain `unavailable`
- mixed-validity populations: pressure whether any future `partial_success` could ever be truthful rather than convenience-based
- item-type-specific structural irregularities: pressure whether mapping or boundary rules collapse on particular item classes or container shapes
- missing auxiliary or partial-package states: pressure whether supposedly excluded surfaces are actually hidden dependencies

If a proposed derivative does not answer one of those named questions more cleanly than another clean acquisition would, do not create it yet.

## 4. Provenance And Sanitization Rules

All new fixtures must follow the existing fail-closed fixture doctrine.

- prefer sanitized derivative packets as the canonical retained baseline
- preserve raw -> sanitized -> negative derivative lineage explicitly when a real project source is involved
- do not keep a raw-derived negative packet as the canonical fixture once a sanitized-derived equivalent exists
- keep raw or user-supplied source archives in `docs/source-lanes/scrivener/source-archive-staging/` only as non-canonical staging material unless an explicit repo decision says otherwise
- require packet-local `README.md` plus `sanitization-report.md` for each accepted baseline
- require packet-local `corruption-note.md` for each negative or irregular derivative
- record platform, observed Scrivener version, source lineage, sanitization posture, intended use, and known limitations in each packet
- preserve structural relationships needed for authority, hierarchy, order, item-type, and correspondence questions
- preserve ambiguity when the ambiguity itself is the evidence question
- remove or replace real prose, private identifiers, and non-essential attachments wherever possible
- when sanitization removes assets or sidecars that might matter later, state that explicitly and do not treat the resulting absence as support proof
- do not treat ownership, publication, or the fact that a project is "real" as support proof
- keep same-source clean and degraded relationships explicit by naming and README cross-reference
- treat every new packet as an evidence asset rather than a hidden implementation shortcut, parser seed, or schema back door

Better fixture provenance does not widen runtime authority by itself.

## 5. Stop Conditions And Blocking Findings

Even after fixture expansion, Stage 2 implementation must remain blocked if any of the following findings remain true:

- mapping remains inconsistent across relevant item types, hierarchy shapes, or version lineages
- manuscript versus non-manuscript boundaries still require labels, titles, compile behavior, workflow meaning, or other semantic guesswork
- degraded states cannot be counted and isolated truthfully, so any future `partial_success` would conceal uncertainty
- compatibility evidence remains too lineage-specific, too version-local, or too migration-narrow to support a bounded implementation slice
- authority plus correspondence still does not support a truthful claim that candidate text-bearing items are extractable
- titles, ordering, folder boundaries, or emitted-unit identity still depend on semantic interpretation rather than explicit structure
- text-side dependency posture remains unclear enough that Stage 2 would smuggle unstable sidecar or application assumptions into Cortex
- sanitization or provenance gaps still obscure the decisive structural question under review
- any extraction-support claim would still outrun the fixture packet actually present

Any one of those findings is enough to keep Stage 2 closed.
